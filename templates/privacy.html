Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 April 2026  |  Version 2.1

GreenField Local Hub ("GLH", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of everyone who uses our platform — customers, producers and visitors alike. This policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the rights you hold under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who We Are

GreenField Local Hub is operated as a cooperative marketplace platform connecting local food producers with customers in the surrounding community. Our registered office is:

GreenField Local Hub Ltd
132 Dartmouth Street, Bletchley, Milton Keynes, MK2 2EF
Email: privacy@glh.co.uk
Phone: 01908 000 100

For the purposes of UK GDPR, GLH is the Data Controller of your personal data.

2. What Data We Collect

We collect personal data in the following categories:

Account & identity data

  • Full name and email address (required to create an account)
  • Phone number and delivery address (required to place orders)
  • Date of birth (where provided, for age-verification purposes)
  • Account role (customer or producer)
  • Hashed password — we never store your password in plain text

Transaction & order data

  • Order history, items purchased, quantities and prices paid
  • Delivery method chosen (collection or home delivery)
  • Delivery addresses associated with each order
  • Loyalty points earned and redeemed

Communications data

  • Messages sent via our Contact Us form (name, email, subject, message)
  • Enquiry records and any follow-up correspondence

Usage & technical data

  • Login timestamps and last-active dates
  • Session cookies required to keep you logged in and maintain your cart
  • Browser type and device type (collected automatically by our web server logs)

Producer data (producers only)

  • Business name and address, product listings, stock levels and pricing
  • Order fulfilment activity associated with your products

We do not collect payment card details directly. All payment processing is handled by a PCI-DSS compliant third-party provider and we receive only a transaction reference.

4. Sharing Your Data

We share data only where necessary and with appropriate safeguards in place:

  • Producers: When you place an order, the producer whose products you purchased receives your name, order details and contact information needed to fulfil that order. Producers are contractually prohibited from using this data for any other purpose.
  • Delivery partners: Where home delivery is selected, your delivery address and contact name are shared with the logistics provider handling that delivery.
  • Payment processor: Your payment details are submitted directly to our payment provider (e.g. Stripe). We receive only a transaction confirmation reference.
  • IT and hosting providers: Our platform is hosted on secure servers. Hosting providers act as data processors under contract and may not use your data independently.
  • Legal authorities: We may disclose data if required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property or safety of GLH, our users or the public.

We do not transfer your data outside the United Kingdom or European Economic Area. Where any sub-processor is based outside the UK/EEA, appropriate transfer mechanisms (e.g. UK Adequacy Regulations or Standard Contractual Clauses) will be in place.

5. Cookies & Session Data

We use cookies and server-side session storage to operate the platform. The cookies we set are:

  • Session cookie (essential): Keeps you logged in and preserves your shopping cart between pages. Expires when you close your browser or after 24 hours of inactivity.
  • CSRF token cookie (essential): Protects form submissions from cross-site request forgery attacks.
  • Flash message cookie (essential): Displays one-time success or error notifications after form submissions.

We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking pixels or analytics cookies at this time. If this changes, we will update this policy and request your consent.

You can configure your browser to refuse or delete cookies; however, disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in or adding items to your cart.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes set out in this policy:

  • Account data: Retained for the lifetime of your account. If you request deletion, your account data is removed within 30 days, subject to any legal hold obligations.
  • Order records: Retained for 7 years from the date of the order to comply with HMRC financial record-keeping requirements.
  • Enquiry records: Retained for 2 years from the date of submission.
  • Server logs (technical data): Retained for 90 days and then automatically deleted.

7. Security

We take security seriously and implement the following measures to protect your data:

  • All passwords are hashed using Werkzeug's PBKDF2-SHA256 algorithm before storage — we cannot read your password
  • All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted via HTTPS/TLS
  • Password reset tokens are generated using HMAC-SHA256 and expire after 1 hour
  • Role-based access control ensures users can only access data relevant to their role
  • Admin and producer routes are protected by server-side authentication checks on every request
  • File uploads are validated for type and size, and stored with randomised filenames to prevent enumeration
  • Our database is not publicly accessible and sits behind firewall rules

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours and inform affected individuals without undue delay.

8. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data. To exercise any of them, please email privacy@glh.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

  • Right of access: Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you (Subject Access Request).
  • Right to rectification: Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data. You can also update most details yourself via Account Settings.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): Ask us to delete your data. We will comply unless we are required to retain it by law (e.g. financial records).
  • Right to restriction: Ask us to pause processing of your data while a dispute is being resolved.
  • Right to data portability: Request your data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g. JSON or CSV).
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Rights related to automated decision-making: We do not currently make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent (e.g. marketing emails), you can withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

9. Children's Privacy

Our platform is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such data, please contact us immediately at privacy@glh.co.uk and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • Display a notice on the platform homepage for 30 days
  • Send registered users an email notification where the changes are significant

Continued use of the platform after the effective date of any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
